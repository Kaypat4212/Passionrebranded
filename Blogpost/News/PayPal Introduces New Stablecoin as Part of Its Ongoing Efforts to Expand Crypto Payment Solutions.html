Fake LayerZero airdrop drains victim's wallets

  • Patrick
  • July 21, 2023

Recently, the security firm Certik issued a warning to users regarding potential airdrop scams.
According to their tweet, fake Layer Zero airdrops are circulating on Twitter through accounts impersonating Layer Zero.
The firm urged caution and advised users not to interact with the suspicious website https://project-layerzero.com which is known to be associated with wallet draining activities.
To ensure users' safety, Certik reminded everyone to stay vigilant and avoid falling victim to such fraudulent schemes. The fake LayerZero website being promoted in the tweets closely resembles the official LayerZero platform.
Once users click on the provided links, they are directed to the deceptive website.
The fraudulent platform then prompts users to connect their cryptocurrency wallets, claiming that this step is necessary to receive the airdrop. However, upon connecting their wallets, the website maliciously drains user's wallets of all their cryptocurrency holdings, resulting in substantial financial losses for those affected.
